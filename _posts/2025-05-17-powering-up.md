# Powering Up the Aetherbeam — Custom 1S2P Li-ion Packs

## 🧠 Overview
This bench session focused on building custom 1S2P lithium-ion battery packs using a precision spot welding setup. While originally intended for a separate project, the configuration proved to be an excellent fit for the Aetherbeam LED system—delivering high capacity in a compact form factor.

## 🎯 Objective
Design and assemble a high-capacity, compact battery pack that:
- Maximizes runtime for LED applications
- Maintains a clean, space-efficient layout
- Ensures safe and reliable operation

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- FNRISI SWM-10 spot welder
- Samsung 35E 18650 lithium-ion cells (3500mAh each)
- Pure nickel strip (for conductivity and durability)
- XT-style balance leads
- 3D-printed battery alignment jig
- Multimeter (for validation and testing)

## ⚙️ Process

### 1. Pack Configuration
A 1S2P configuration was selected:
- 1 series group (nominal 3.7V)
- 2 cells in parallel for increased capacity

This results in a combined capacity of approximately **7000mAh**, ideal for extended runtime applications.

### 2. Cell Preparation & Alignment
- Cells were inspected and matched for consistency
- A custom 3D-printed jig was used to maintain alignment during assembly
- Layout was optimized for both compactness and structural stability

### 3. Spot Welding Assembly
- Nickel strips were spot welded to create solid electrical connections
- Heat exposure to cells was minimized compared to traditional soldering
- Weld consistency ensured reliable conductivity across the pack

### 4. Lead Integration
- XT-style leads were prepared and positioned for clean integration
- Connections were verified prior to final assembly

## 🚧 Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge:** Maintaining precise alignment during welding  
**Solution:** Designed and used a custom 3D-printed jig to hold cells securely in place

**Challenge:** Avoiding heat damage to lithium-ion cells  
**Solution:** Used spot welding instead of direct soldering to reduce thermal stress

**Challenge:** Balancing compact design with usability  
**Solution:** Optimized layout to fit within Aetherbeam constraints without sacrificing accessibility

## 📊 Results / Outcome
- Achieved ~7000mAh capacity using Samsung 35E cells
- Clean, compact battery pack suitable for integration into Aetherbeam
- Reliable weld quality and consistent electrical performance
- Improved projected runtime for LED applications

## 🔄 Improvements / Next Steps
- Integrate full BMS for enhanced protection
- Design a sealed enclosure for outdoor/weather resistance
- Explore modular battery swapping options
- Release STL files for the battery jig and enclosure
